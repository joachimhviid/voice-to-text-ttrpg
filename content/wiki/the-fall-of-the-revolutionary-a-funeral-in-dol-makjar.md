## Chronicle

The sprawling city of Dol-Makjar, nestled at the eastern edge of Kahad, is dominated by the massive Guardian Wall, a memorial erected to the heroes of the Shapers' War. This grand edifice, adorned with statues of various races—orcs, dwarves, elves, and humans—serves as a constant reminder of the region’s long history of heroism and conflict. The heart of the action centers in The Rookery, a whimsical neighborhood defined by its vertical towers, sky bridges, and conical shingled dwellings, serving as a haven for poets, performers, and storytellers. Within this locale, the life of Halandil Fang is anchored; his modest but expansive home on the hill is a working sanctuary for art and artistry, a backdrop for the unfolding tragedy. 

The catalyst for the current day is the execution of Thjazi Fang. Under the watchful gaze of the assembled crowds—nobles, lords, and ladies of the Sundered Houses—Thjazi is marched toward the gallows. His arrest and condemnation are framed by official decrees, yet the moment is fraught with complex emotions: a mix of expected celebration, profound sorrow, and simmering dissent. The appearance of the Revolutionary Guard, specifically Azune Nayar, adds a layer of arcane tension. Azune, displaying his authority as an Arcane Marshal, intervenes with arcane rites to delay the inevitable, utilizing *Detect Magic* to locate a subtle ward placed upon Thjazi. The initial confrontation, though brief, marks a profound moment of farewell, punctuated by exchanges of cryptic messages and lingering familial affection between Halandil and his brother. 

After the execution, the atmosphere shifts into a somber wake, transforming the viewing into a gathering of mourners and confidantes. The sheer weight of the event—a revolutionary figure being condemned by the nobility—is palpable. The arrival of multiple characters, each with their own histories and vested interests in Thjazi's fate, only heightens the sense of fractured community. Later, the investigation shifts to the hidden mechanisms of the old tannery, where a discovery of blood, magical residue, and the ransacked, occult nature of the hidden chamber hints at a far deeper conspiracy. The recovery of the enigmatic Stone of Nightsong, an artifact connected to the Shapers' War and elven lore, becomes a focal point of both investigation and suspicion. The culmination sees the revelation of the planned escape—a glyph designed for teleportation—and the deep mystery surrounding the true nature of Thjazi's fate and the conspiracy that surrounds his death, all framed by questions of love, betrayal, and the enduring power of memory.

## Summary

- The ceremony begins with the public execution of Thjazi Fang under the gaze of the assembled lords and ladies of Dol-Makjar.
- Azune Nayar, the Arcane Marshal, intervenes magically during the execution, buying time and attempting to ward off a lingering ward.
- The aftermath leads to a somber wake at the Fang family home, where a core group of friends and family gathers to mourn and analyze the conspiracy surrounding Thjazi’s death.
- During the investigation of Thjazi's hideout in the tanneries, the party discovers a crucial, unexplained glyph and the remnants of a violent struggle.
- The discovery of the Stone of Nightsong, an ancient elven artifact, and the final reveal of the comprehensive escape plan provide major plot hooks for the next campaign arc.
