## Chronicle

The day of the execution of Thjazi Fang arrived under a canopy of gray. In the bustling, vertical quarter of The Rookery, life seemed to proceed in a strange rhythm, momentarily disrupted by the solemn gravity of the occasion. Azune Nayar, an Arcane Marshal of the Revolutionary Guard, watched the approaching wagon carrying the celebrated—and condemned—thief, arcanist, and murderer. The crowd was a mixture of lords, ladies, and citizens from the Sundered Houses, all gathered under the monumental Guardian Wall that marked the eastern edge of Dol-Makjar.

Minutes before his arrest, Azune connected with Thjazi through a discreet magical message. The exchange was charged with coded farewells and hints of arcane wards. Later, Azune reinforced this connection, detecting a minor conjuration and illusionary ward hidden in Thjazi’s coat. As the execution commenced, Azune ensured that a potent ward was placed upon the prisoner. The man’s final moments were a flurry of panicked messages and whispered plans, concluding with his fateful step through the door.

The subsequent scenes unfolded within the warmth of Halandil Fang's home in The Rookery. The lingering sorrow of a wake was palpable, as Shadia, Thjazi’s intended receptacle, waited patiently. The arrivals of old friends and companions—Olgud Akarat, and later, the solemn theories of the late and departed—provided a mixture of comfort, professional banter, and profound melancholy. The true nature of the gathering, however, was exposed to all: a careful investigation into the mysteries of Thjazi's life.

At the heart of the house, Azune Nayar found himself in a moment of intense contemplation, remembering both the fighting spirit of the old campaigner and the lifeless man before him. Azune performed the rites, carrying Thjazi’s body into the prepared recess. The final moments were marked by a blend of ritual observance and escalating conspiracy. The true nature of the mystery was illuminated by the visit of a wild, mysterious figure, Vaelus, of the Sisters of Sylandri, who arrived in a carriage bearing the emblems of the Candescent Creed, bringing with him the physical evidence of Thjazi's passing.

The ensuing investigation proved that Thjazi’s final moments were not simply a spectacle of justice, but the climax of a complex, multi-layered conspiracy. The discovery of the *Stone of Nightsong*, a powerful elven artifact, and the subsequent understanding of the clandestine operations within the tannery confirmed that the execution was a carefully orchestrated piece of theatre. The subsequent arrival of new players—including the formidable and enigmatic presence of the demon, Tyranny—further unsettled the peace. The revelations spiraled into a dizzying, emotional confrontation, leading to the profound sense that the true threads of the conspiracy remained tangled and unbroken.

## Summary

- Azune Nayar oversaw the public execution of Thjazi Fang at the Guardian Wall of Dol-Makjar, despite attempts by the condemned man to escape.
- Within Halandil Fang's home, the investigation of the deceased revealed a complex pattern of hidden conspiracy, marked by arcane wards and ceremonial rites.
- Multiple intriguing characters arrived, including the mysterious Vaelus of the Candescent Creed, whose presence intensified the sense of underlying magical mystery.
- Key relics, such as the *Stone of Nightsong* and Thjazi's hidden glyph, were examined, suggesting a deeper, unrevealed plot connected to the city's arcane underbelly.
- The episode concluded with the realization that the execution was not the end, but merely the dramatic opening to a much larger, unresolved conflict involving multiple noble houses and ancient magical powers.
