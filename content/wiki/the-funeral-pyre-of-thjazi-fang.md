## Chronicle

The city of Dol-Makjar, nestled at the edge of Kahad, possesses a distinct character, marked by the enduring spirit of heroism, rebellion, and profound history. The physical manifestation of this history is the massive Guardian Wall, which flanks the eastern edge of the metropolis, memorializing the Shapers' War with towering statues of orcs, dwarves, elves, humans, halflings, gnomes, and beastfolk. The immediate area of the proceedings was the Fang family home in The Rookery, a neighborhood known for its quirky, interconnected towers and artistry, a center for poets, performers, and bards. The air was thick with the mingled scents of magic, expensive liquors (particularly *Yahrgraz*), and the solemn, sweet fragrance of jasmine.

***

**The Day of Judgment**

The chronicle began under a pall of gray mist, drawing the gathered populace—lords, ladies, and common folk—to the gallows constructed beneath the Guardian Wall. The air was charged with anticipation and dread as the wagon arrived, bearing the man sentenced to death: Thjazi Fang. Despite the solemnity of the occasion, his presence was marked by a handsome, almost flippant orcish smile. Azune Nayar, an Arcane Marshal of the Revolutionary Guard, approached the prisoner with the utmost professionalism, commencing a Detect Magic ritual. He paused, his eyes briefly connecting with Thjazi, offering a moment of bought time to any desperate final messages. The prisoner, however, remained defiant, asserting that his words were his own to give. 

Azune, sensing the need for more than a simple magical sweep, employed his full mastery of the spell, casting Detect Magic. The magic flared, illuminating a subtle, hidden ward on Thjazi's coat—a delicate piece of trickery meant for a daring escape. Azune followed protocol: a nod to Halandil, a gesture indicating the ward's location, and the urgent, private message that Halandil knew his brother was capable of escape. As the herald read the charges—profaning arcanism, theft, skullduggery, murder, and sedition—Thjazi, in one last, visible effort, broadcasted messages to Halandil, urging him to help Murray and the Penteveral, confirming his ongoing life of making mischief. His last words to the crowd were a defiant proclamation that the false authority would fall. With a final burst of magical energy and the sharp *snap* of the noose, he was taken away, leaving behind a torrent of unanswered questions.

**Mourning and Memory**

The focus shifted swiftly to the Fang residence, where the initial rites were performed. Amidst the controlled chaos of a funeral wake, Azune Nayar gently carried Thjazi's body inside. The sheer formality and wrongness of the scene were palpable. The gathered family and friends, including Thaisha Lloy, were in a state of guarded grief. Later, the arrival of veteran warrior Teor Pridesire caused a momentary, charged encounter with Azune and Halandil. The day descended into a rich tapestry of reunions: the introduction of the new arrivals—Sir Matthew Davinos and Lady Aranessa—culminated in the most emotionally charged confrontation. Aranessa, despite her attempts at maintaining dignity, was visibly crumbling under the weight of memory, her accusations of betrayal and neglect echoing loudly. The most significant magical discovery came in the form of a legendary artifact: the Stone of Nightsong, a relic of the Shaper Goddess Sylandri, found hidden within the wreckage of Thjazi's secret hideout, requiring a confluence of powerful magical investigation to unearth.

The gathering climaxed with the arrival of a final figure: Vaelus, an elf from the Sisters of Sylandri. His arrival, alongside a series of increasingly dramatic magical revelations (including the revelation of a mysterious celestial lineage), forced the assembled guests to confront the true depth of the conspiracy, leaving the question of who, or what, orchestrated Thjazi's demise—and the fate of the Stone of Nightsong—hanging heavy in the scented, sorrowful air.

## Summary

- The proceedings centered on the execution and death of the rogue arcanist, Thjazi Fang, under the authority of the Revolutionary Council in Dol-Makjar.
- The magical investigation conducted by Azune Nayar revealed a ward on Thjazi, hinting at a planned escape, but he ultimately met his fate at the gallows.
- At the Fang family residence, the wake became a nexus of charged reunions, marked by emotional outbursts, the discovery of the mysterious Stone of Nightsong, and the confrontation between groups over Thjazi's life and passing.
- Several key revelations unfolded: the failure of Thjazi's elaborate escape plan, the identity of the Stone of Nightsong as a sacred elven relic, and the existence of a deeply layered conspiracy surrounding his death.
- The chronicle concludes with a profound sense of unresolved mystery, setting the stage for future investigations into the true nature of the political corruption and magical threats plaguing Dol-Makjar.
