# The Raven's Shadow and the Stone of Nightsong

## Chronicle

The conclusion of the funeral rites and the investigation into the missing artifact, the Stone of Nightsong, reveal that Thjazi Fang's demise was far from natural. Following the initial shock of the execution, several characters—including Azune Nayar, Teor Pridesire, and Laura Bailey (Thimble)—are drawn into the chaotic scene within the old Fang family home, The Rookery, in the neighborhood of Dol-Makjar. The investigation reveals the presence of a magically concealed hiding spot (the 'glyph' site) and evidence of a complex conspiracy. The key mystery centers on the Stone of Nightsong, a powerful elven artifact that was entrusted to Thjazi. The players establish several deep connections and conflicts: the lingering tension between the nobility (represented by the Sundered Houses and the Halovar family) and the revolutionaries; the mystery surrounding Thjazi's final moments and the true nature of his death; and the sudden arrival of various influential, and sometimes hostile, figures who converge at the site of the loss, culminating in a promise of deeper mysteries and further confrontation in the following session. The episode concludes by having the surviving characters commit to finding the stolen artifact and uncovering the truth of Thjazi's death.

## Summary

- Following the death of Thjazi Fang, the emotional center of the Fang family home, the players become involved in a forensic examination of the scene, searching for answers about his demise.
- The investigation uncovers signs of sophisticated magical rigging, specifically the presence of a magically concealed glyph, indicating that Thjazi's death was meticulously planned and not a spontaneous act of justice.
- The players gather key information about the Stone of Nightsong, an ancient elven artifact, and its potential importance to the wider magical and political conflicts of Dol-Makjar.
- The convergence of various noble houses and magical factions points to a complex conspiracy involving elements of the Sundered Houses, the Revolutionary Guard, and the secret histories of the city's magic.
- The central mystery remains unanswered: who engineered Thjazi's death, why was the artifact targeted, and how does this connect to the broader, ancient conflicts of the continent?
